Where Finmo wins

This prototype borrows openly. Each of these companies does one thing better than anyone, and the build takes the lesson. What none of them can do is the reason Finmo exists.

CompanyWhat they do best (and what I took)Where they stop
BrexControls that finance teams trust. Took the approval workflow: payments over a threshold need a second person, and the preparer cannot release their own payment.US only, card led. No cross border rails, no licences outside the US.
RampProving value in money terms. Took the savings card: FX margin avoided, wire fees avoided, treasury yield, with the method shown.US only, spend first. Fused software with an account, but cannot move money across borders.
WiseTreating a transfer like a parcel. Took payment tracking: staged progress, an honest arrival estimate, and a delay flagged before the customer asks.An account, not a finance OS. No opinion on whether you should pay, or what it does to next month.
The gap none of them cross. Brex and Ramp can think and hold money, in one country, on cards. Wise can move money everywhere, without judgment. Finmo is treasury first, cross border, on its own licences in five jurisdictions, with an AI that proposes actions and rails that actually settle them. The others recommend without executing, or execute without recommending. This demo closes that loop: every insight, from an overdue invoice to an FX exposure, ends in a button that moves real money.
